What you can access for free

The BIS SDMX API is public and does not require authentication for released statistical datasets. It exposes:

International Banking Statistics
Credit to Private Sector
Debt Securities Statistics
Property Price Statistics
Exchange Rates
Derivatives Statistics
Central Bank Statistics
Financial Stability Indicators

through SDMX REST APIs.

As an individual sitting at home, you can do exactly the same discovery that professional economists do.

----------------
First 30 Minutes: Discover Dataflows

In SDMX, a Dataflow is equivalent to:

"A dataset family"

Examples:

International Banking Statistics
Credit Statistics
Property Prices
Exchange Rates

Your first task should be:

import sdmx

bis = sdmx.Client("BIS")

flows = bis.dataflow()

or, depending on the library version:

flows = bis.get("dataflow")

Then inspect:

for k in list(flows.dataflow.keys())[:20]:
    print(k)

Your goal:

Create a dataframe:

flow_id	flow_name
CBS	Credit Statistics
IBS	International Banking Statistics
...	...

This becomes your catalog.

Dataflow discovery is the SDMX equivalent of browsing database tables.

Next 30 Minutes: Discover Structures (DSDs)

Pick one interesting dataflow.

For example:

flow = flows.dataflow["CBS"]

Retrieve its Data Structure Definition (DSD).

The DSD tells you:

Dimensions
Measures
Time dimensions
Allowed codes

Think of it as:

DESCRIBE TABLE

for SDMX.

Next 30 Minutes: Build a Metadata Explorer

Create a dataframe like:

Dimension	Description
REF_AREA	Country
TIME_PERIOD	Date
FREQ	Frequency
MEASURE	Indicator

This immediately tells you:

Which countries exist
Which indicators exist
Which frequencies exist

without downloading a single observation.

This is the most useful thing for your SDMX-Discovery project.
